What is a sacrament?

The seven sacraments are Holy Orders, Matrimony, Anointing of the Sick, Eucharist, Reconciliation, and Baptism. The giving of Divine life occurs via the Sacraments, that are powerful symbols of grace that were established by Christ and given to the Church.

Why is baptism important?

Your commitment to Jesus Christ is demonstrated through your baptism. It serves as a formal declaration of your commitment to and belief in Jesus Christ. It is an essential cornerstone of the Christian life and comes after salvation via repentance and faith.

What are the requirements for Baptism?

What are the items associated with baptism?

Water is utilised as a symbol of life during baptism. New life is given by Christ through baptism. Holy oil is a representation of power and healing. The chrism is used to consecrate and approve a person's baptism into the Church's large family. It is an aromatic oil that has been blessed. Additionally, the catechumens' oil has significant symbolic significance. The baptised are really hailed as defenders of the religion and defenders of Christendom. During a baptism, a white gown is presented as a sign of new life and the new dignity that the baptised individual possesses. Before joining the other believers in the Church, people who had been baptised in the past were required to put on a fresh, white garment. In the belief that Christ, the light of the world, may illuminate the child and enable those who love and support him or her to walk besides them in the faith, the candle is presented to the parents or the godfather of the baptised person.

What happens during Baptism?

1. The baby, godparents and parents are called up.
2. The priest reads from the Bible
3. The baby's parents and godparents take oaths, confess their religion and the faith they wish the child to be raised in, and renounce Satan and wickedness.
4. I baptise you in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, the minister says as he pours water over the infant's head (in Orthodox Churches, the baby is briefly put completely under the water)
5. A lit candle may occasionally be presented to the godparents to symbolise the presence of Jesus in the infant's life.
6. Parents might put their child in a beautiful white outfit for the ceremony.

How do we know baptism has been recieved?

The baptism is considered to have taken place once the recipient comes in contact with the water.
